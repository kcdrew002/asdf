Your Nutrition

Good nutrition allows your brain and body to function so that you can become the best version of yourself. 

Benefits of Good Nutrition

Nutrients allow your body to grow and repair itself as you take on daily tasks. Your diet determines how much energy you'll be working with throughout the day so its important to consider all the foods you're eating. From little snacks to sizeable meals, it all makes a difference. 

Help Your Heart

Having fruits, vegetables, and whole grains in your diet reduce your risk of heart disease and boosts your energy.

Growing Strong

Working calcuim into your diet will help your teeth and bones stay strong. Dairy products, fish, and dark leafy greens are rich in calcium and should be considered for a healthy diet. 

Stay Hydrated

Staying properly hydrated will keep you from experiencing the effects of dehydration.These effects include slowed metabolism, joint pain, muscule fatigue, and dizziness. Staying hydrated is one of the easiest ways to support your body.

Diets For Losing Weight

The Flexitarian Diet

This diet can be thought of as a flexible vegetarian diet. The majority of your diet will be plant based but you can still eat meat every so often. Consider starting out with 2 meat-less days a week and working your way up to 5 over time. 

learn more

Mediterranean Diet

This diet is based in fruits, vegetables, whole grains, nuts, seeds, and the healthy fats found in foods like avocados and olives. You'll want to avoid processed foods and limit your sugar intake to reap the benefits of this diet. 

learn more

Intermittent Fasting 

The focus of intermittent fasting is on when you eat rather than what you eat. A popular application of this diet is the 16/8 method which give you an 8 hour window to eat and 16 hours to fast. It's important to make healthy food decisions during your 8 hours so you have the energy you need. It's also important to stay hydrated especially during your fast. 

learn more

Low-carb Diets

Low-carb diets restrict your carbs and focus more on protein rich foods. When your body is low on carbs, it begins to use fatty acids for energy in a process known as ketosis. 

learn more

Gaining Weight in a Healthy Way

3,000-calorie Diet

If you're looking to gain more weight you're going to have to consume more calories than your body can burn. You'll want to focus on eating carbs, fat, and protein to gain weight. Avoid highly processed foods and sugar since those are still unhealthy.
